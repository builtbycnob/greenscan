Architecture
From Competitor Websites to Your Phone

The system runs automatically 3 times a day. No human intervention needed — it collects, filters, analyzes, and delivers.

🔍

Collect

Scans 30 competitor websites, blogs, newsrooms, and press release pages. Also reads RSS feeds and searches Google for mentions on third-party sites (weekly).

~3-5 min
🧹

Deduplicate

Removes content already seen in previous scans. Typically filters out 40-60% of results, so the AI only processes genuinely new information.

~1 sec
🤖

Classify with AI

Each new signal is analyzed by AI, which assigns a category (product launch, partnership, hiring, expansion…), a relevance score (1-5), a summary, and extracts named entities (companies, people, products).

~30-60 sec
💾

Store

All classified signals are saved to a database — building a searchable history of competitor activity over time.

~1 sec
📋

Generate Brief

AI compiles the most relevant signals (score ≥ 3) into a structured Battlefield Brief: executive summary, signals grouped by category, key takeaways, and suggested actions.

~10-20 sec
📱

Deliver to Telegram

The brief lands in the founder's Telegram every morning. Critical signals (score 5) trigger an immediate alert — don't wait for the next brief.

~2 sec

What We Cut
And Why It's the Right Call
🖥️

Web Dashboard

The founder reads the brief on Telegram, not on a dashboard. A frontend only makes sense if the brief isn't enough — and we won't know that until it's running.

💬

Natural Language Search

"Who is responsible for PepsiCo sustainability in Argentina?" — valuable, but requires building an AI-to-database query engine plus a UI. Phase 2: add this as a Telegram bot command instead.

📧

Email Delivery

Telegram covers 100% of the use case. Adding email means another service to configure, another failure point, and zero additional value.

🔗

Cross-Signal Correlation

The team already flagged this as highest technical risk. In the brief, AI can spot basic patterns through prompting alone — no custom correlation logic needed.

👥

Contact Management

Without a frontend, there's no place to manage contacts. They're still extracted and saved in the database — ready for Phase 2.

Risk Register
Known Risks & Mitigations

These have been verified as of March 2026. No assumptions — all limits checked against official documentation.

High

AI request limits lower than expected

The primary AI service allows 1,000 requests/day (not 14,400 as initially estimated). With ~100 requests per scan × 3 scans/day = 300/day, we fit — but with little margin. Mitigation: batch 10 signals per request, use backup AI for overflow.

High

Competitor URLs may be broken or blocked

Testing revealed 2 of 3 initial URLs returned errors. Some competitor websites actively block automated access. Mitigation: test every URL before adding, maintain fallback sources (RSS, Google search results).

Medium

Google search credits are one-time, not monthly

The 2,500 free search queries are a lifetime allowance, not renewable. At 30 queries/week, they last ~19 months. Mitigation: acceptable for MVP, plan budget for credits after depletion.

Medium

Scheduled tasks may be paused on inactive repositories

GitHub disables scheduled jobs after 60 days without a code commit. Mitigation: at least 1 commit/month or trigger runs manually as backup.

Low

Scheduled scans can be delayed 5-29 minutes

The scheduler is not guaranteed to run exactly on time. Mitigation: for a daily brief this is completely acceptable — the founder reads it at 9 AM regardless.

Low

Telegram messages have a 4,096 character limit

Long briefs need to be split across multiple messages. Mitigation: automatic splitting is built into the delivery module.

Low

Database cold starts after inactivity

The free database goes to sleep when unused and takes 0.5-2 seconds to wake up. Mitigation: negligible — the pipeline runs for several minutes anyway.
